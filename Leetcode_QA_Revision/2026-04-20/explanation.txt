Title: Two Furthest Houses With Different Colors
URL: https://leetcode.com/problems/two-furthest-houses-with-different-colors/

LLM Explanation:
The problem asks for the maximum distance between two houses of different colors. The distance between house `i` and house `j` is `abs(i - j)`.

The core idea is that the pair of houses that are furthest apart must include at least one of the houses at the ends of the street (i.e., house at index 0 or house at index `n-1`). This is because for any pair of interior houses `i` and `j` (where `0 < i < j < n-1`), the distance `j-i` is always smaller than the distance from an endpoint, like `j-0` or `(n-1)-i`. We can prove that a better or equal distance can always be found by pairing with an endpoint.

This simplifies the problem significantly. We only need to consider two scenarios for the maximum distance:
1.  The distance between the first house (index 0) and the furthest house from it that has a different color. To maximize this distance, we should find the rightmost house (largest index `j`) such that `colors[j] != colors[0]`. The distance is `j - 0`.
2.  The distance between the last house (index `n-1`) and the furthest house from it that has a different color. To maximize this distance, we should find the leftmost house (smallest index `i`) such that `colors[i] != colors[n-1]`. The distance is `(n-1) - i`.

The final answer is the maximum of these two calculated distances.

This approach elegantly handles all cases:
- If `colors[0]` and `colors[n-1]` are different, the rightmost house different from `colors[0]` is house `n-1` (distance `n-1`), and the leftmost house different from `colors[n-1]` is house `0` (distance `n-1`). The result is `n-1`, which is the maximum possible.
- If `colors[0]` and `colors[n-1]` are the same, the algorithm finds the first house from each end with a different color and computes the maximum possible distance involving an endpoint.

The implementation involves two simple loops: one scanning from the right to find the furthest house different from `colors[0]`, and another scanning from the left to find the furthest house different from `colors[n-1]`.

- **Time Complexity:** O(n), as we iterate through the array at most twice in the worst case.
- **Space Complexity:** O(1), as we only use a few variables to store indices and the length of the array.
