Title: Minimum Jumps to Reach End via Prime Teleportation

Question:
You are given an integer array nums of length n . You start at index 0, and your goal is to reach index n - 1 . From any index i , you may perform one of the following operations: Adjacent Step : Jump to index i + 1 or i - 1 , if the index is within bounds. Prime Teleportation : If nums[i] is a prime number p , you may instantly jump to any index j != i such that nums[j] % p == 0 . Return the minimum number of jumps required to reach index n - 1 . Example 1: Input: nums = [1,2,4,6] Output: 2 Explanation: One optimal sequence of jumps is: Start at index i = 0 . Take an adjacent step to index 1. At index i = 1 , nums[1] = 2 is a prime number. Therefore, we teleport to index i = 3 as nums[3] = 6 is divisible by 2. Thus, the answer is 2. Example 2: Input: nums = [2,3,4,7,9] Output: 2 Explanation: One optimal sequence of jumps is: Start at index i = 0 . Take an adjacent step to index i = 1 . At index i = 1 , nums[1] = 3 is a prime number. Therefore, we teleport to index i = 4 since nums[4] = 9 is divisible by 3. Thus, the answer is 2. Example 3: Input: nums = [4,6,5,8] Output: 3 Explanation: Since no teleportation is possible, we move through 0 &rarr; 1 &rarr; 2 &rarr; 3 . Thus, the answer is 3. Constraints: 1 &lt;= n == nums.length &lt;= 10 5 1 &lt;= nums[i] &lt;= 10 6
