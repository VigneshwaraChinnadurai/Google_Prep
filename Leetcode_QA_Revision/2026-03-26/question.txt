Title: Equal Sum Grid Partition II
Difficulty: Hard

Question:
You are given an m x n matrix grid of positive integers. Your task is to determine if it is possible to make either one horizontal or one vertical cut on the grid such that: Each of the two resulting sections formed by the cut is non-empty . The sum of elements in both sections is equal , or can be made equal by discounting at most one single cell in total (from either section). If a cell is discounted, the rest of the section must remain connected . Return true if such a partition exists; otherwise, return false . Note: A section is connected if every cell in it can be reached from any other cell by moving up, down, left, or right through other cells in the section. Example 1: Input: grid = [[1,4],[2,3]] Output: true Explanation: A horizontal cut after the first row gives sums 1 + 4 = 5 and 2 + 3 = 5 , which are equal. Thus, the answer is true . Example 2: Input: grid = [[1,2],[3,4]] Output: true Explanation: A vertical cut after the first column gives sums 1 + 3 = 4 and 2 + 4 = 6 . By discounting 2 from the right section ( 6 - 2 = 4 ), both sections have equal sums and remain connected. Thus, the answer is true . Example 3: Input: grid = [[1,2,4],[2,3,5]] Output: false Explanation: A horizontal cut after the first row gives 1 + 2 + 4 = 7 and 2 + 3 + 5 = 10 . By discounting 3 from the bottom section ( 10 - 3 = 7 ), both sections have equal sums, but they do not remain connected as it splits the bottom section into two parts ( [2] and [5] ). Thus, the answer is false . Example 4: Input: grid = [[4,1,8],[3,2,6]] Output: false Explanation: No valid cut exists, so the answer is false . Constraints: 1 &lt;= m == grid.length &lt;= 10 5 1 &lt;= n == grid[i].length &lt;= 10 5 2 &lt;= m * n &lt;= 10 5 1 &lt;= grid[i][j] &lt;= 10 5
